- **description:** Delivers independent news journalism, classical music, and alternative programming to Coloradans through a statewide network of radio stations and digital platforms. CPR News provides in-depth reporting on Colorado politics, the environment, and communities; CPR Classical and Indie 102.3 serve music listeners across the state.
- Colorado Public Radio traces its roots to KCFR, which launched on the University of Denver campus in 1970 — Colorado's first public radio station, built by a small team committed to independent journalism at a time when public broadcasting was still finding its footing nationally.
- **impact:**
  - 760,000+ monthly radio listeners statewide
  - 1 million+ digital consumers each month
  - ~90% of Colorado's population reached through the statewide broadcast network
  - 15 full-power stations and 17 translators across the state
- **location_description:** Headquartered in Centennial with studios and newsrooms across Colorado, broadcasting to 90% of the state's population.
