# About Page Copy Drafts

Two versions for an "About / How It Works" page linked from the nav. Goal: explain what GoodLocal is, why it exists, and convert curious visitors into first-time donors.

---

## Version A — Brand Voice (polished, calm, values-forward)

### Give locally. Give with confidence.

GoodLocal is a giving platform built for Denver — a single, trusted place to discover local nonprofits, donate in minutes, and see your impact over time.

---

### Why GoodLocal exists

Most people who want to give locally run into the same wall: they don't know where to start.

National charity sites feel generic. Individual nonprofit websites can be hard to evaluate. And when a moment of generosity strikes — a news story, a walk through your neighborhood — there's rarely an easy, trustworthy way to act on it.

That gap costs Denver's local nonprofits a lot. Not because people don't care, but because the friction is real: Is this organization legit? Is my $50 going to actually help someone? What have I even given to over the last year?

GoodLocal was built to answer those questions — not by pressuring you to give more, but by making it easier to give *better*. We surface vetted local nonprofits, explain who they are and what they do, and give you one place to track your giving over time.

We believe giving should feel confident, sustainable, and close to home — not like a marketing moment.

---

### How it works

**1. Discover**
Browse the map or filter by cause — housing, food access, education, youth programs, and more. Every organization listed is a registered nonprofit based in and focused on Colorado.

**2. Donate**
Click Donate on any charity page. A secure donation window opens in-place — no redirect, no new accounts to create. Pay by card or Apple Pay. GoodLocal never touches your payment; donations go directly to the nonprofit via Every.org.

**3. Track**
Once you create a free GoodLocal account, your giving history lives in one place. No more hunting through email receipts. See what you've supported, build a giving habit that works for your budget, and come back when you're ready to give again.

---

### Our commitment to you

GoodLocal does not profit from donation amounts and does not handle your money. We are a discovery and tracking tool — not a fundraising platform. Our incentive is your trust, not your transaction.

## Version B — Personal / Informal (from Nick, founder voice)

### I built this because I couldn't find it anywhere else

### GoodLocal is a local giving platform for Denver. Discover nonprofits near you, donate easily, and keep track of where your money actually went.

---

### Why this exists

A few years ago I started wanting to give more intentionally — not to huge national organizations, but to places actually doing work in my city. Neighborhoods I live near. Problems I see.

But I had no idea where to start. I didn't know which nonprofits were operating near me. I didn't know which ones were trustworthy. And even when I did donate somewhere, I'd forget about it — a receipt in my inbox and nothing else.

I asked around. Most people I know feel the same way. They want to give locally. They're just not sure how, and the friction quietly stops them every time.

So I built GoodLocal.

It's not a fundraising campaign. It's not trying to guilt you into giving. It's just a clean, honest tool to help people who already want to give — actually follow through, find the right orgs, and keep track of what they've done.

---

### Here's how it works

You open the map, pick a cause you care about — food access, housing, kids, animals, whatever — and you see what's near you. Every organization on here is a real, registered nonprofit focused on Colorado. Click one to learn more. If it feels right, donate. It takes about 60 seconds.

If you create a free account, your donations are tracked in one place. So six months from now, you can look back and actually see what you've contributed — not just feel vaguely good (or vaguely guilty) about it.

Payments go through Every.org, a trusted nonprofit payment processor. GoodLocal doesn't touch your money and doesn't profit from your donations. That's intentional.

---

### Who this is for

If you're a Denver professional who's been meaning to give more locally but never quite found the right org, the right moment, or the right tool — this is for you.

If you want to give in a way that feels real and trackable rather than reactive and forgotten — this is for you.

You don't need to give a lot. You just need somewhere to start.
