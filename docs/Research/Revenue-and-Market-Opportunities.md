# Revenue and Market Opportunities

*Who has a demonstrated appetite to pay for charitable giving infrastructure — beyond the obvious workplace benefit angle.*

---

## Competitor Quick Reference

- **Benevity** — The dominant enterprise CSR platform used by Fortune 500 companies like Google and Microsoft; starts at $100K+/year and requires a dedicated CSR team to operate, making it completely inaccessible to SMBs.
- **YourCause (Blackbaud)** — The second-largest enterprise platform, strong in healthcare and higher ed through Blackbaud's existing nonprofit software relationships; same structural inaccessibility to small businesses as Benevity.
- **Groundswell** — The most SMB-friendly platform in the market at $4,500/year entry, using a DAF-based model where employees get personal giving accounts; no local curation and a national focus with no Denver-specific angle.
- **Millie** — Consumer-forward workplace giving tool with a "dating app" style nonprofit discovery experience (3 matched orgs per week based on location); custom pricing estimated at $10K–$25K+/year, and while local-aware, it has no Denver-specific curation.
- **Deed** — A YC-backed platform targeting tech companies that combines matching with volunteering and skills-based giving; charges a 2.5% disbursement fee on all donations and runs enterprise minimums that exclude most SMBs.
- **EarthShare** — The cheapest option in the market at $1–2 per employee per month, with the fee deducted from donation payouts rather than invoiced; limited to environmental nonprofits and offers minimal employer reporting or discovery tools.
- **ColoradoGives.org (Colorado Gives Foundation)** — The dominant local giving platform in Colorado, a 501(c)(3) that runs Colorado Gives Day ($56.6M raised in 2024 from 4,500+ nonprofits); free for nonprofits to list, funded by corporate sponsorships and voluntary donor transaction fees rather than employer subscriptions.
- **Double the Donation** — Nonprofit-facing tool that helps charities identify and surface matching gift eligibility to their existing donors; not an employer-side platform and not a direct competitor.

---

## Context

The corporate giving platform market shifted heavily B2B because companies are more motivated to pay than individual donors — employer brand, retention signaling, and HR budgets create a clear buyer. But that's not the only paying customer segment. This doc explores the full landscape of who pays for what in the charitable giving ecosystem, and where GoodLocal has a realistic angle.

---

## Segment 1: Local Business Cause Marketing

### The Opportunity

Denver restaurants, gyms, retail shops, and service businesses running "5% of October revenue goes to [local nonprofit]" campaigns — powered by GoodLocal. The business pays a platform fee for the marketing value and community credibility. The nonprofit gets a donation stream. GoodLocal earns platform revenue without touching donated funds.

This is completely unclaimed by national platforms. Benevity and YourCause are not selling to a Capitol Hill coffee shop. The local-first framing is native to this customer type in a way it isn't for the generic employer benefit pitch.

### What Businesses Get

- A credible, hosted giving campaign page ("We support Warren Village")
- Featured placement on GoodLocal alongside their chosen nonprofits
- A shareable marketing asset — social posts, window sticker, email newsletter copy
- Donation tracking and an end-of-campaign impact summary to share publicly

### Revenue Model

| Tier | Price | What's Included |
|---|---|---|
| **Community Supporter** | $99/month | Featured nonprofit badge, campaign page, basic impact summary |
| **Giving Partner** | $249/month | + Monthly giving campaign tools, co-branded promotion to GoodLocal donors, social assets |
| **Founding Partner** | $499/month | + Prominent homepage and category placement, quarterly impact report, priority support |

50 businesses at $199/month average = **~$120K ARR**. Sales cycle is weeks, not months. Decision is made by the owner or a single marketing person.

### Why It Works for GoodLocal

- Aligns with the "local community identity" positioning in a way employer benefits don't
- No payment infrastructure needed beyond what's already built (Every.org handles donations)
- Creates a network effect: businesses bring their own audiences to GoodLocal
- Authentic — a Denver brewery featuring "we support a local youth arts program" is a more genuine use case than an employer checking a benefits box

### Realistic Assessment

**High near-term potential.** This is the fastest path to B2B revenue that doesn't require enterprise sales cycles, HR relationships, or complex product builds. The competitive moat is local presence and trust — something no national platform can replicate.

## Segment 2: Financial Advisors and Wealth Managers

### The Opportunity

Advisors increasingly manage clients' philanthropic portfolios alongside investment portfolios — helping allocate DAF funds, identify causes, and plan charitable giving. The DAF market alone processed **$7.7 billion in grants in 2024** (DAFgiving360 alone, up 25% YoY). Over 4,600 independent advisors work with DAFgiving360.

The problem: when a client says "I want to give $50K to Denver food security organizations," the advisor has no professional tool for that. They Google or call the community foundation. GoodLocal's curated, vetted Denver database with cause tagging and financial health indicators is exactly what they need — and nothing in the market currently serves this at the local level.

### What Advisors Get

- A professional-grade local nonprofit discovery tool to use in client meetings
- Curated lists by cause area, neighborhood, and organization size
- Financial health and transparency indicators for due diligence conversations
- A shareable format — "here are 5 Denver food security orgs worth considering for your giving plan"
- Integration potential with DAF platforms (Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable, community foundation DAFs)

### Revenue Models

**Option A — Advisor subscription:** $99–$299/month per advisor seat. Targets RIAs and independent financial planners in Denver/Colorado who have philanthropic clients.

**Option B — API/data licensing:** GoodLocal's vetted nonprofit database licensed to regional DAF platforms or wealth management firms as a local discovery layer embedded in their own tools. One enterprise license deal = more revenue than dozens of individual subscriptions.

**Option C — Transaction facilitation:** If an advisor directs a DAF grant through GoodLocal, a 1–2% facilitation fee is standard market rate (Benevity charges 2.9%). This conflicts somewhat with the "we don't touch the money" principle and should be evaluated carefully.

### Relevant Players

- **Ren (formerly Renaissance Charitable):** Dominant white-label DAF infrastructure for wealth management firms; serves hundreds of firms; no local discovery layer
- **Foundation Source:** Branded DAF programs for financial advisors; focused on back-office, not discovery
- **Daffy:** Consumer DAF at $3/month; launched "Daffy for Work" but no local curation

None of these serve the "help me find the right Denver nonprofits for my client" use case.

### Realistic Assessment

**Highest revenue ceiling, longest sales cycle.** A single API licensing deal with a regional DAF platform or enterprise subscription with a Denver RIA firm could generate more revenue than dozens of consumer subscriptions. The path requires enterprise B2B development — not product-led growth. Worth pursuing in parallel with other channels once GoodLocal has nonprofit data quality that can stand up to advisor scrutiny.

## Segment 3: Community Banks and Credit Unions

### The Opportunity

Colorado community banks and credit unions have **Community Reinvestment Act (CRA)** obligations requiring them to invest in low-to-moderate income communities. They also have genuine marketing motivation to differentiate on local community investment. FirstBank literally co-sponsors Colorado Gives Day — the appetite to pay for local giving infrastructure is demonstrated.

A white-label "give to local Denver nonprofits" feature embedded in a community bank's checking account or app is a plausible B2B contract. The bank gets a CRA-credible feature and a marketing differentiator; GoodLocal provides the nonprofit database, donation infrastructure, and a branded discovery layer.

### What Banks Get

- A turnkey "community giving" feature they can offer customers
- A curated, vetted list of Denver nonprofits (not 1.5M generic options)
- Donation facilitation via Every.org's existing rails
- Reporting for CRA documentation purposes
- Co-branded marketing opportunity ("Give Local with [Bank Name] + GoodLocal")

### Revenue Model

SaaS license to the financial institution: **$2,000–$10,000/month** depending on their customer base and feature scope. Alternatively, a per-transaction model (small fee on each donation processed through their branded interface).

### Relevant Denver-Area Targets

- **Ent Credit Union** (Colorado Springs / Denver metro, 500K+ members)
- **ColoEast Bankshares**
- **Colorado's Credit Union**
- **Elevations Credit Union** (Boulder/Denver)
- Any Colorado community bank post-FirstBank/PNC transition looking to differentiate on local identity

### Realistic Assessment

**Medium potential; long sales cycle.** Financial institutions move slowly, have compliance requirements, and have long vendor evaluation processes. However, the CRA budget is real and allocated annually. Worth pursuing after GoodLocal has a proven consumer product and at least one marquee employer or business partner as a reference. A single bank contract at $5K/month = $60K ARR.

## Segment 4: Nonprofits (Paid Discovery Tier)

### The Opportunity

Nonprofits do spend on donor acquisition — the average nonprofit allocates ~$13,000/year to paid advertising (up 12% in 2024) and direct mail. The question is whether GoodLocal can capture some of that spend as a more efficient local donor acquisition channel.

**Important constraint:** Charging nonprofits for basic listing is a non-starter. Every competitor (ColoradoGives, Charity Navigator, GreatNonprofits) offers free listings. Paywalling nonprofits kills the supply side of the platform. Any nonprofit monetization must be above a free baseline.

### What Nonprofits Could Pay For

- **Featured/sponsored placement** in search results and category pages ($50–$200/month)
- **Donor analytics** — "47 new donors clicked through to Every.org from your GoodLocal profile last month" ($99–$299/month)
- **Profile enhancement tools** — A/B testing headlines, managing donor reviews, showcasing impact updates (bundled with analytics tier)
- **Campaign boosting** — Promoted placement during Colorado Gives Day, year-end, and other peak giving moments (one-time campaign fee)

### Realistic Assessment

**Low initially; medium at scale.** This only works once GoodLocal has meaningful donor traffic. A nonprofit won't pay $100/month for featured placement if 50 people/month visit the platform. The nonprofit monetization path is a second-order priority — it becomes viable as a result of consumer and B2B traction, not as an early revenue source. Worth building toward but not worth prioritizing before donor-side traction exists.

## Segment 5: Colorado Gives Day / Giving Day Ecosystem

### How ColoradoGives.org Works

Colorado Gives Foundation is a 501(c)(3) — not a for-profit SaaS. Its revenue comes from:
- **Corporate sponsorship** (FirstBank/PNC presenting sponsor; estimated $500K–$2M/year)
- **Donor transaction fees** (2.05% mandatory + 1.94% optional fee that 92% of donors cover voluntarily)
- On $54.6M raised in 2024, transaction fees alone generate ~$2.2M (before the platform partner Mightycause's cut)

4,533 nonprofits listed in 2024. It is the dominant local giving discovery platform in Colorado.

### GoodLocal's Angle

Not direct competition — complementary. ColoradoGives is statewide and giving-day-focused. GoodLocal is Denver-specific and year-round. ColoradoGives has no map-based discovery, no proximity search, no editorial curation, no community event context.

The play: **become the year-round discovery layer that feeds Colorado Gives Day** — building donor intent and nonprofit familiarity throughout the year, then converting around December. A formal co-marketing arrangement where GoodLocal-discovered nonprofits see increased Colorado Gives Day donations (and vice versa) is more valuable than trying to compete.

Direct revenue from giving day organizers is low. The strategic leverage is high — being embedded in the Colorado giving ecosystem as the discovery layer positions GoodLocal without a head-on fight with a well-capitalized 501(c)(3).

## Segment 6: Community Foundations (Strategic Partner, Not Customer)

The Denver Foundation (~$4M/year in grants), Rose Community Foundation, and Colorado Gives Foundation are not realistic paying customers. Their administrative margins are thin, they have existing vendor relationships, and they're not in the business of purchasing nonprofit directories.

The realistic path is **strategic partnership**: foundations promoting GoodLocal to their DAF holders and nonprofit grantees in exchange for preferred integration or co-branded content. A small capacity building grant ($20K–$50K) from The Denver Foundation's Capacity Building Fund is plausible — but that's one-time funding, not a revenue model.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Local business cause marketing is the fastest unclaimed B2B path.** Short sales cycle, local moat, no enterprise complexity, aligns with GoodLocal's community identity positioning. Start here alongside employer giving.

2. **Financial advisors are underserved and high-value.** Nobody is serving "help me find the right Denver nonprofits for my philanthropic client." The data quality and curation GoodLocal is building has direct value in that conversation. Pursue as a medium-term enterprise channel.

3. **Nonprofit monetization requires consumer traction first.** Don't build a nonprofit paid tier until you have enough donor traffic to make the value proposition real. It's a natural second-order revenue stream, not an early priority.

4. **Colorado Gives Day is a partner, not a competitor.** Align with it. Become the year-round layer that makes December more effective. A formal relationship with Colorado Gives Foundation is more valuable than any transaction fee revenue from trying to compete.

5. **Never take a cut of donations in any model.** "We don't touch the money" is GoodLocal's most defensible trust claim. Every revenue model should charge for platform access, data, or tools — never a percentage of what flows to the charity.
